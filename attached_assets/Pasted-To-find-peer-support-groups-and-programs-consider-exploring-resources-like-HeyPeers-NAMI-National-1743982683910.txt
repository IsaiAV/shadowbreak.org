To find peer support groups and programs, consider exploring resources like HeyPeers, NAMI (National Alliance on Mental Illness), and Mental Health America. These organizations offer online and in-person support groups, educational programs, and resources for individuals and families dealing with mental health conditions. 
Here's a breakdown of resources and programs:
HeyPeers:
HeyPeers offers a platform for connecting with peer supporters and finding support groups, including chatrooms and video meetings, led by trained peer supporters. 
NAMI (National Alliance on Mental Illness):
NAMI | National Alliance on Mental Illness provides NAMI Connection, a free, peer-led support group for adults with mental health conditions, and NAMI Peer-to-Peer, a free, eight-session educational program. 
Mental Health America:
Mental Health America offers a list of organizations and resources, including support groups for various mental health conditions and related issues. 
Other Organizations and Programs:
Anxiety and Depression Association of America: Offers a list of support groups across the country for various mental health conditions, including PTSD. 
Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance (DBSA): Provides online support groups for people living with depression and bipolar disorder. 
International OCD Foundation: Offers support groups and treatment groups for individuals with OCD and related conditions. 
Postpartum Support International (PSI): Provides online support groups for parents who have experienced stillbirth, early infant loss, or other postpartum challenges. 
SMART Recovery: A program that aims for abstinence through self-empowerment and self-directed change. 
Alcoholics Anonymous (AA) and Narcotics Anonymous (NA): Offer support groups for those with alcohol and drug addiction. 
Support Groups Central:
Curates support groups from other organizations, such as NAMI and re:MIND, allowing you to search for groups by topics such as depression, addiction, LGBTQ+, and even COVID-19. 
Mayo Clinic:
Provides information on the benefits of support groups and how they can help individuals connect with others who have similar experiences. 
SAMHSA (Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration):
Offers resources and information on peer support, including a definition of peer support and its different forms. 
988 Lifeline:
Provides 24-hour, toll-free, and confidential support to anyone in suicidal crisis or emotional distress.

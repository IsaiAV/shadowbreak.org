Field Distortion Engine Documentation

Overview

The Field Distortion Engine (FDE) is a modular text analysis system that integrates multiple analytical approaches to detect and measure narrative distortion, emotional manipulation, and linguistic patterns associated with deception or coercion.

The engine processes text through five specialized modules, then combines their outputs to calculate the field curvature (λ∇Ψ), a single scalar value representing the degree of narrative distortion. Based on this value, the text is classified into one of three categories: Stable Field, Distorted Field, or Recursive Collapse.

Fractal Drift Detector (FDD)

The Fractal Drift Detector identifies recursion and metaphor reuse patterns in text. It analyzes how narrative elements repeat and transform throughout a document, revealing potential manipulation through repetitive language structures.

Key Metrics:
  • Recursion Score: Measures the degree of self-similarity in non-adjacent text segments
  • Narrative Stability: Quantifies the consistency of narrative direction
  • Fractal Signatures: Identifies recurring patterns and their transformations
  • Metaphor Clusters: Detects figurative language patterns and their frequency

High recursion scores may indicate manipulation through repetitive reinforcement, while low narrative stability suggests potential discrepancies or inconsistencies.

Narrative Entropy Scanner (NES)

The Narrative Entropy Scanner measures semantic entropy (disorder) over time in a text. It identifies areas of high cognitive dissociation, narrative trauma zones, or emotional collapse within the discourse.

Key Metrics:
  • Lexical Entropy: Disorder in word choice and frequency distribution
  • Syntactic Entropy: Disorder in sentence structure and grammatical patterns
  • Semantic Coherence: Consistency of meaning across adjacent sentences
  • Entropy Gradient: Rate of change in entropy between segments

High entropy segments may indicate emotional flooding, cognitive dissonance, or deliberate obfuscation. Sudden entropy spikes often mark narrative trauma zones where coherence breaks down.

Symbolic Density Analyzer (SDA)

The Symbolic Density Analyzer identifies symbol-heavy language and metaphor clusters, mapping emotional loading and potential manipulation through symbolic association and framing.

Key Metrics:
  • Symbol Density: Concentration of symbolically charged language
  • Emotional Blackholes: Segments with extremely high symbolic density
  • Symbol Clusters: Related symbolic elements appearing together
  • Grooming Patterns: Language patterns associated with manipulative behaviors

Symbol-heavy language may indicate emotional manipulation, while certain symbol clusters can reveal unconscious associations or deliberate framing attempts.

Echo Pattern Engine (EPE)

The Echo Pattern Engine detects repeated phrases that appear in contextually distorted settings, revealing potential grooming loops, obedience reinforcement, or false memory implantation.

Key Metrics:
  • Echo Count: Number of significant repeated phrases detected
  • Echo Intensity: Strength of echo patterns based on repetition and context shift
  • Context Similarity: How similar the surrounding context is for each repetition
  • Context Shifts: Significant changes in surrounding context for repeated phrases

Phrases repeated in increasingly distorted contexts may indicate manipulative reinforcement, gaslighting, or attempts to normalize aberrant concepts through repetition.

Observer Simulation Layer (OSL)

The Observer Simulation Layer inserts analyst personas into the input stream to test resonance response and simulate "observer interference" for feedback loop modeling.

Key Metrics:
  • Resonance Scores: How strongly the text resonates with different analytical perspectives
  • Feedback Loops: Potential reinforcement patterns that amplify over iterations
  • Sentiment Shifts: Significant emotional transitions across the narrative

This module helps identify which analytical perspective is most suited to the text, and reveals potential feedback mechanisms that could amplify distortion over time.

Field Curvature Model

The Field Curvature Model combines outputs from all modules to calculate λ∇Ψ (Lambda Del Psi), a scalar value representing the overall degree of narrative distortion.

λ∇Ψ = Δσ + εᵥ - Φ(μ)

  • Δσ (Delta Sigma): Sentence variance/field tension (from symbolic density)
  • εᵥ (Epsilon): Trauma echo intensity/emotional amplitude (from echo patterns)
  • Φ(μ) (Phi Mu): Lexical uniqueness factor/entropy reduction (from narrative entropy)

This formula balances field tension (potential for distortion), emotional amplitude (intensity of distortion), and entropy reduction (resistance to distortion) to produce a single value measuring narrative field stability.

Interpretation Guide

Field classifications provide a general assessment of narrative stability and potential manipulation:

Stable Field (λ∇Ψ < 0.2)
  • Coherent narrative
  • Minimal distortion
  • Low manipulation risk
  • Standard communication
Distorted Field (0.2 ≤ λ∇Ψ < 0.6)
  • Moderate distortion
  • Potential manipulation
  • Some inconsistency
  • Requires investigation
Recursive Collapse (λ∇Ψ ≥ 0.6)
  • Severe distortion
  • High manipulation
  • Recursive patterns
  • Potential coercion

For detailed analysis, examine the individual module outputs to identify specific patterns and areas of concern within the text.
